Ochei takes APC and INEC to court over Delta Primary elections

Category: News

Posted: October 20th

Victor Ochei called for the cancellation of the APC primary election in Delta

Ochei claimed the party and INEC did not use the approved list of delegates
He asked that a fresh primary be conducted.
Victor Ochei who is the former speaker of the Delta state House of Assembly has called on Abuja division of the Federal High Court to nullify the governorship primary election of the All Progressives Congress. The election was held on September 30 and according to Vanguard, the former speaker listed the APC, the Independent National Electoral Commission and Chief Great Ogboru who was declared winner, as defendants.
The plaintiff, through his counsel, Ahmed Rahi, called on the court to declare that “the APC governorship primary election that held on September 30, was illegal and unlawful on the ground that delegates not known to law were used for the exercise.” He noted that the high court had in a judgment that was delivered by Justice Anwuri Chikere on June 19, “authenticated a list of delegates of APC in Delta state.” He alleged that the list was side-lined by the party during the primary election



Consequently, he urged the court to nullify the purported primary election for being unlawful, unjust and deliberately carried out in disregard of subsisting judicial directive. He also asked the conduct to compel the electoral agency to conduct a fresh election with the approved delegate list.

Recall that violence erupted at the Delta North senatorial district primary of the All Progressives Congress (APC) on Wednesday, October 3, leading to the death of two persons.
According to Vanguard, there were varying accounts of what transpired; with some sources claiming the victims were killed by police stray bullets at the Federal College of Education, (Technical) where the exercise was being conducted.
Others, however, said the victims were killed by political thugs.
Eristicminds.org gathers that the incident was confirmed by the institution’s Student Union speaker, Vincent Ubani, who identified one of the victims as Henry Nweke, a 200 level student of Industrial Technical Education.

Ubani, however, could not identify the second corpse; and both bodies have been deposited at the mortuary.

Meanwhile, Dr Emmanuel Uduaghan, the immediate past governor of the state, clinched the party’s ticket for the Delta South senatorial district. The former governor expressed happiness with the process and stated that the APC would emerge victorious in the 2019 general elections.
